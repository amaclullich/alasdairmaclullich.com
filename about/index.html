About

About Alasdair MacLullich

I am a geriatrician and delirium researcher based in Edinburgh. My work centres on the recognition, assessment and care of people with delirium, alongside acute geriatric medicine and orthogeriatrics.

My work

I am Professor of Geriatric Medicine at the University of Edinburgh and work clinically in acute geriatric medicine and acute orthogeriatrics at the Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh.

Alongside clinical and academic work, I am Editor-in-Chief of Delirium and Chair of the Scottish Hip Fracture Audit. I co-founded the European Delirium Association.

I led the original development of the 4AT delirium assessment tool in 2011 and remain responsible for its updates.

The websites linked from this site cover several distinct kinds of work: information for patients and families, clinical assessment tools, professional education, academic publishing, writing and public awareness.

Areas of work

  • Delirium recognition, assessment and care, including the 4AT.
  • Acute geriatric medicine and orthogeriatrics, including hip fracture care.
  • Research, clinical education, public information and scientific publishing.
